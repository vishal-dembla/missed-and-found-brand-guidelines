1. Brand Overview

Brand name: missed and found.

Tagline: Templates for fresh start

Mission: To help people without a clear playbook find clarity and confidence through thoughtfully designed, functional tools starting with resume templates.

Tone: Calm, clear, empathetic, professional, understated.

2. Brand Personality

  • Quiet confidence > never loud or flashy.
  • Minimal and balanced > ample white space and breathing room.
  • Human and warm > small imperfections add authenticity.
  • Premium yet approachable > think The Ordinary, Kinfolk, or Aesop.

3. Typography

  • Headings: Playfair Display (serif).
  • Body: Inter (sans-serif).
  • Font sizes: Name 18-22 pt, Headings 12–14 pt, Body 10–11 pt.
  • Use bold and spacing rather than color for hierarchy.

4. Colors

Use the full brand palette:

Cream #FAF7F2 – background.
Charcoal #2B2B2B – primary text.
Warm Grey #6B6B6B – secondary text.
Soft Sage #B8C5B4 – accents or borders.
Accent #D4A574 – hover states or subtle highlights.

5. Resume Template Design Guidelines

File and Format

  • Software: Microsoft Word (.docx).
  • Editable: All text must be user-editable.
  • ATS-friendly: Avoid columns or text boxes.

Layout

  • Margins: 0.75 – 1 inch.
  • Alignment: Left-aligned text, ragged right edges.
  • Spacing: 1.2 – 1.4 line spacing.
  • Hierarchy: Clear levels for headings and body.

6. Product Mockup and Listing Image Guidelines

Goal

Make each product look calm, premium, and purposeful. Visuals should convey quiet confidence and clarity.

Image Types

  • Main thumbnail: Flat-lay of the resume on cream background with small brand mark.
  • Feature slide: List 3–5 benefits (ATS-friendly, easy to edit, professional design).
  • Context mockup: Resume on desk or laptop with soft lighting and minimal props.
  • Testimonial slide: Italic quote and small brand mark.
  • How it works slide: Icons or text showing included files.

Visual Style

  • Backgrounds: Cream or white; no gradients.
  • Lighting: Soft, natural.
  • Composition: Centered or off-center with negative space.
  • Props: Minimalist, neutral tones.
  • Overlay text font: Inter (400–500).
  • Logo use: Lowercase "missed and found." in Playfair Display.
